Financial Summary

Over the last two seasons, the team has improved its possession by 12%, reflecting stronger control in midfield and improved build-up play. However, this progress has been accompanied by a decline of 8% in attacking efficiency, indicating missed opportunities in the final third and a need for more clinical finishing and better chance conversion. From a financial perspective, the club invested 60M€ in transfers while generating 35M€ in income through player sales. This results in a Return on Investment (ROI) of 58%, suggesting a balanced but improvable transfer strategy. Optimizing recruitment through data-driven scouting and targeted signings could enhance both sporting performance and financial sustainability in upcoming seasons.
